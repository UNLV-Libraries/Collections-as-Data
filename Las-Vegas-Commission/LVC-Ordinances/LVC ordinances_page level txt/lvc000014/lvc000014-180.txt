EMERGENCY ORDINANCE
ORDINANCE NO. 271

AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND SECTION 9 OF ORDINANCE NO. 165 OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS, ENTITLED
"AN ORDINANCE TO PROHIBIT GAMING AND OPERATION OF SLOT MACHINES IN THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
WITHOUT FIRST OBTAINING A LICENSE THEREFOR REGULATING THE SAMS, FIXING THE AMOUNT OF SUCH
LICENSE, PROVIDING A PENALTY THEREFOR, REPEALING ORDINANCE NOS. 77, 82, 88, 103 and 115,

AND ALL ORDINANCES AND PARTS OF ORDINANCES IN CONFLICT THEREWITH".

The Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, do ordain as follows:

Section 1. Section 9 of Ordinance 165 of the City of Las Vegas is hereby amended to read
as follows:

Any licensee holding a valid existing license from the City of Las Vegas for a current
quarter, may, during such current quarter, secure a license for additional slot machines,
games or devices, as the case may be, ever and above the particular number of slot machines,
games or devices for which he, they or it holds a license, for the remainder of the quarter
by paying the city clerk the license fee above provided, for the additional particular slot
machine, game or device, as the case may be, provided that such licensee first be required
to make an application to the Board of City Commissioners of Las Vegas for such additional
slot machine, game or device and receive the approval of the Board of City Commissioners
at their regular meeting, after which the city clerk shall issue an additional license
for the remainder of such quarter, and the additional license shall be upon the same terms
and conditions as though issued under the provisions of section 8 of this ordinance, as
amended by Ordinance No. 245.

SECTION 2. This ordinance is declared to be and is hereby passed as an EMERGENCY ORDINANCE
and it shall take effect immediately after its passage and publication.

SECTION 3. The City Clerk is hereby authorized to have this Ordinance published in the
Las Vegas Age, a weekly newspaper printed and published in the City of Las Vegas, County of
Clark, State of Nevada, for a period of two weeks, that is to say once each week for a
period of two weeks.

The foregoing Ordinance was read aloud for the first time and voted upon at a regular meeting
of the Board of City Commissioners on the 6th day of April, 1942.

Vote for: Commissioners: Smith, Tinch, Clark, Rubidoux, and his Honor, the Mayor, Howell
C. Garrison, and was read aloud for the second time and passed by the unanimous consent of
the Board as an emergency Ordinance on the 6th day of April, 1942.

Vote for: Commissioners: Smith, Tinch, Clark, Rubidoux and his Honor, the Mayor, Howell C.
Garrison.

Voting aye: - Noes, none, Absent, none.

Approved this 6th day of April. Howell C. Garrison

Mayor

Attest: Helen Scott, City Clerk
LeRoy Whipple, Deputy.
