An ordinance entitled:	AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND ORDINANCE NO. 712 TO REGULATE AND CONTROL THE

POSTING AND MAINTAINING OF OUTSIDE ADVERTISING SIGNS OF MOTELS, MOTOR COURTS, HOTELS, AND
LIKE ESTABLISHMENTS; PROVIDING PENALTIES FOR THE VIOLATION THEREOF; AND REPEALING ALL ORDI-
NANCES IN CONFLICT HEREWITH, was read by title and referred to Commissioners Bunker and
Sharp for recommendation.

Commissioner Fountain introduced and moved the adoption of the following resolution:

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners deem it advisable to promote industrial
development within the area, and

WHEREAS, it appears that industrial development can be augmented if there is
land available.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas, Nevada go on record as favoring the introduction of a Bill by Congress per-
mitting the State of Nevada to acquire certain Government Land in Eldorado Valley for the
purpose of industrial development.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that copies of this resolution be forwarded to the
Nevada Congressional delegation.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and Mayor Baker voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Whipple introduced and moved the adoption of the following resolution:

WHEREAS, many Hungarian refugees have arrived in the United States after
their escape from Russian tyranny, and

WHEREAS, these refugees will become American citizens in due course, and

WHEREAS, Katherine and Tommy Rodnoti are the first to arrive in Southern

Nevada.



NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas at a regular meeting thereof held on the 16th day of January, 1957 that Katherine
and Tommy Rodnoti are welcomed to the City of Las Vegas by the Board of Commissioners and
the citizens of Las Vegas and are extended the privileges of all its citizens.

Motion seconded by Commissioners Fountain and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and Mayor Baker voting aye; noes, none.
