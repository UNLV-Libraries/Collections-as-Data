City Manager Kennedy stated that Urban Renewal Warrant Nos. 9442 -
9444, in the amount of $4,951.95, represented the amount the
Government had. put into Urban Renewal.

Commissioner Fountain moved Urban Renewal Warrant Nos. 9442 - 9444,
in the amount of $4,951.95, be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote
Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Gragson
voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.

Commissioner Whipple moved the minutes of December 2, 1959 be approved
by reference, and the Mayor and City Clerk be authorized to sign same.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote
Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Gragson
voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.

Commissioner Fountain moved the application of Joseph A. Gordon for
license for eight slot machines at The Keg Room, 2365 E. Bonanza,
be approved, on the basis of favorable police report, and the
Supervisor of License and Revenue authorized to issue the license.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor
Gragson voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.

Commissioner Fountain moved the application of Edward Levinson for
two additional slot machines at The Horseshoe Club, 116 N. Second
Street, be approved, on the basis of favorable police report, and the
Supervisor of License and Revenue authorized to issue the license.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor
Gragson voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.

City Manager Kennedy stated the Police Department requested that the
application of Robert D. Lawrence for a 50% interest in the Tavern
License at Patrick's Peyton Place, 1110 Fremont, present owner G.
Patrick Kennedy, not be acted on at this time, but that action on
this matter be deferred until the next meeting, pending receipt by
the Police Department of additional information which had been
requested by it.

Commissioner Fountain moved this application be held in abeyance
until the next meeting.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor
Gragson voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.

City Manager Kennedy informed the meeting he was in receipt of an
application from Tommy Smith for a 5% interest in the Gaming license
of the California Club; that this application was cleared through and
approved by the State Tax Commission but did not appear on the
agenda as it had only been received yesterday. He also advised that
there was a favorable police report on the applicant.

Commissioner Fountain moved the application of Tommy Smith for a 5%
interest in the gaming license of the California Club be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor Mayor
Gragson voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Elwell.
