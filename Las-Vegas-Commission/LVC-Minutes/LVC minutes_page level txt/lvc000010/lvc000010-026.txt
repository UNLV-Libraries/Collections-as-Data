Commissioner Whipple moved that lots 1 to 4, Block 2; Maryland Tract No. 2
be rezoned from C-1 to R-4 and that Lot 5, Block 2, Maryland Tract No. 2
remain as C-2.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Com-

missioners Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes,
none.

Commissioner Whipple moved that Lots 16 - 17; Block 23; Wardie Addition; and
Lots 31 - 32, Block 24, Pioneer Heights be referred back to the Planning
Commission with the recommendation they be rezoned from R-4 to C-2.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Com-

missioners Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

City Manager Kennedy stated that there was a revolving fund to purchase fire
hydrants, and he would like to get authority to purchase another 100 at an
approximate cost of $11,000.

Commissioner Bunker moved that the recommendation of the City Manager be accepted
and the purchase of 100 fire hydrants at a cost of $11,000 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Fountain and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes
none.		

Mr. Kennedy said that he had been in contact with the Nevada Armored Transport
and that for $40.00 a month they will pick up the city bank deposit and take the
parking meter nickles to the bank daily. He further stated that they were covered
by $1,000,000 insurance on their cargo.

Commissioner Fountain moved that the City Manager be instructed to employ the

Nevada Armored Transport at a cost of $40.00 per month to transport the City's
bank deposits.
