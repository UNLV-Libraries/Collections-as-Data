Mr. Earl Turman appeared before the Board stating that he had bought out all of the
interest of his partner, Willie Robertson, in the Chicadee Club, and requested that Mr.
Robertson's name be omitted from the license. He also asked approval of his application
for an additional "21" game.

Commissioner Sharp moved that Mr. Turman's application for deletion of Willie Robertson
from the gaming and liquor licenses of the Chicadee Blub be approved, and that he be
granted a license for one additional "21" game.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

Commissioner Sharp moved that the Mayor be authorized to sign a complaint on the
condemnation of the alley behind the Dollar Market.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

Commissioner Sharp moved that the escrow instructions for the proposed fire station site
on the SE corner of Utah and Industrial be changed to an option to purchase, and the
Mayor be authorized to sign the necessary papers for the purchase of this property on
May 15, 1955.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.
