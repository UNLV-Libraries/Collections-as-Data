Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, Commissioner Bunker.

At this time Mr. Jack Conlon of the Tavern Owners Association appeared before the Board
at this time and stated that he thought the ordinance should be clarified so that
package liquor licenses could not be changed to tavern licenses and vice versa. He
also requested that the Board consider changing the ordinance limiting the number of
licenses.

Mr. Theodore Glenn also appeared before the Board to protest the issuance of a package
liquor license at 2301 South 5th, said building which is right in front of his motel.

He filed a letter of protest with the Board.

City Engineer George Wade presented a realignment of South 3rd, Utah and 5th Street at
this time. He stated that it was the recommendation of the Traffic and Parking Committee
that this intersection be changed so that the traffic comes in at right angles.

Thereafter Commissioner Sharp moved this street design be accepted as recommended by
the City Engineer and Traffic and Parking Committee.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

City Manager George Rounthwaite recommended that the bid of Gene's Auto Body for the
towing contract for the City of Las Vegas in the amount of $210.00 per month be accepted.

Commissioner Bunker moved the recommendation of the City Manager be accepted and this
contract awarded to Gene's Auto Body.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.
