OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

April 29, 1929.

At a recessed regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of
Las Vegas, Clark County, Nevada, held on April 29th, 1929, at the hour of three o'clock,

P. M.



PRESENT: His Honor the Mayor, J. F. Hesse, Commissioners W. B. Mundy and O. J. Smith,
together with the City Attorney and Clerk.

ABSENT:	Commissioners W. C. German and Roy Neagle.

Meeting called to order by the Mayor.

Thereafter, upon motion duly made, seconded and carried, the meeting was adjourned
to Tuesday, May 2nd, 1929, at the hour of three o'clock, P. M.

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

May 2nd, 1929.

At a regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas,

Clark County, Nevada, held on May 2nd, 1929, at the hour of three o'clock P. M.

PRESENT: W. B. Mundy, Mayor Pro tem, Commissioner O. J. Smith, together with
the City Attorney and Clerk.

ABSENT: His Honor the Mayor, J. F. Hesse and Commissioners W. C. German and Roy
Neagle.

Meeting called to order by Mayor Pro tem, and immediately recessed until the hour
of seven-thirty o'clock, P. M.

7:30 P. M.

PRESENT: W. B. Mundy, Mayor Pro tem, Commissioners O. J. Smith and W. C. German,
together with the Engineer for the City, City Attorney and Clerk.

ABSENT: His Honor the Mayor, J. F. Hesse and Commissioner Roy Neagle.

Meeting called to order by Mayor Pro tem.

All claims allowed as shown by Claims and Warrants Register on file in the office of
the Clerk; reports read and approved.

Upon motion duly made, seconded and carried it was ordered that monies collected by the
City Clerk, Municipal Judge and Plumbing Inspector, for licenses, fines and fees respectively,
totaling $4833.00, be apportioned as follows:- To the Police & Fire Fund, $2575.00; to the
General Fund, $2375.00; to the Water, Sewer & Light Fund, $83.00.

The Clerk presented the application of Rumph & Morgan, (Cactus Club), for a license for
the operation of two gaming tables. The fee of One Hundred Fifty Dollars ($150.00) having
been paid and such application having been approved by the Police and Fire Commissioners, upon
motion duly made, seconded and carried, it was ordered that the license be issued.

The Clerk presented the application of John A. Scott, (Arena Beer Garden), for a
license for the operation of two gaming tables. The fee of One Hundred Fifty Dollars ($150.00)
having been paid and such application having been approved by the Police and Fire
Commissioners, upon motion duly made, seconded and carried, it was ordered that the license
be issued.
