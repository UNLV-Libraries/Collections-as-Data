CALL

TO VIOLA BURNS, CITY CLERK OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS:

A special meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas is hereby
called, to be held at the Board Room of said Board in the City Clerk's office, in the City of
Las Vegas, State of Nevada, to be held on Monday the 11th day of September, 1933, at the
hour of three o'clock P.M. of said day, to consider and act upon the following business:

1.	To consider the acquisition of a right of way on Clark Avenue in said City, from
Main Street in said City to the Westerly limits thereof.

2.	To consider and ordinance regulating slaughtering of animals, etc;

Dated this 11th day of September, 1933.

E.W. Cragin_______

Mayor of the City of Las Vegas.

Minutes of a Special called meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las
Vegas, held on the 11th day of September, 1933 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

The following answered roll call, Mayor E.W. Cragin Commissioners Arnett, Mundy, German
and Down, City Attorney Henderson and City Clerk.

At this time the Mayor of the City called the meeting to order stating that the meeting
was called for the purpose of considering whether or not it would be advisable for the City to
accept the proposition of the Los Angeles and Salt Lake Railroad Company whereby said Railroad
Company offers to deed to the City a certain right of way over certain parcels of land situate
in the western portion of said City and which right of way embraces a parcel of land on Clark
Avenue running from Main Street in said City to the Russell property in said City, and

WHEREAS, the Railroad company offers to give and grant unto said City the right of
way for the construction of a street along the portion of said City hereinabove re-
ferred to, and offers to grant an easement to the said City to cross its railroad
tracks on said street, to-wit: Clark Avenue, and

WHEREAS, in consideration of the said Railroad Company giving and granting to said
City said parcel of land for a right of way, and said easement over its railroad
tracks, the Railroad Company desires that the City agree to construct and maintain
the said street over and across the easement of said railroad tracks approximately
one hundred (100) feet long by eighty (80) feet wide,

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the said Railroad Company's granting to said
City right of way and easement, free to cost to said City,

BE IT RESOLVED, that the City of Las Vegas, for and in consideration of said
tract of land for said right of way, and in consideration of said easement, does
hereby agree to and with said Railroad Company to construct and maintain said
street over, across, and on that portion of said street over which the railroad
tracks of said Railroad Company now run, to-wit: a portion of said street ap-
proximately one hundred (100) feet long by eighty (80) feet wide: Providing how-
ever that the City in agreeing to construct and maintain said designated portion
of said street, approximately one hundred (100) feet long by eighty (80) feet
wide, shall in no manner assume any liability or be liable from any cause what-
