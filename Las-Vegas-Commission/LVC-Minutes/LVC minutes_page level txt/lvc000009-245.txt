Las VegaS; Nevada
July 13, 1954

(CALL OF SPECIAL MEETING)

A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas held this 13th
day of July, 1954, was called to order at the hour of 4:00 P.M. by His Honor Mayor Baker
with the following members present:

Mayor	C. D. Baker

Commissioner	Wendell Bunker

Commissioner	Rex A. Jarrett

Commissioner	Harris P. Sharp

Commissioner	Reed Whipple

City Manager	George Routhwaite

Asst. City Mgr.	R. W. Notti

City Clerk	Shirley Ballinger

Absent:	City Attorney	Howard W. Cannon

The application of Marie R. and Carl E. Phillips for a Restaurant Beverage license at the
Charleston Inn, 17 W. Charleston Blvd; formerly issued to Ronald E. McGarry was considered
by the Board at this time.

Thereafter Commissioner Jarrett moved the application of Marie R. and Carl E. Phillips for
a Restaurant Beverage license at the Charleston Inn, 17 W. Charleston be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker; Jarrett; Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

At this time Howard W. Cannon; City Attorney arrived at the meeting.

The request of Mr. William Coulthard on behalf of Mr. Charles Lasky for the issuance of
a building permit for a proposed store and office building at Cincinnati and South 5th
Street was considered by the Board.

Mr. Russell Taylor representing the American Legion and the tenants of their property
and Mr. Thomas Foley representing Mr. Theodore Glenn; owner of the Glenn-Vegas Motel
appeared and again protested the granting of this permit on the grounds that there was
not sufficient parking facilities provided.

Commissioner Jarrett moved that this application for a building permit be deferred until
July 21 to give Mr. Lasky an opportunity to secure a definite confirmation of his lease
with Terrible Herbst for parking facilities.

This motion died for lack of a second.

Thereafter Commissioner Bunker moved that this permit be granted providing that some
arrangement be made through the Engineering Department to allow for as much parking on
Cincinnati as is possible and that Mr. Lasky's building be set back 4 feet to allow
60° angle parking on the North side of Cincinnati and parallel parking on the south side
of this street.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker; Jarrett, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noeS; none. Not voting Commissioner
Sharp.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time; this special
meeting adjourned.
