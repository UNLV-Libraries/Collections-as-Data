RESOLUTION Liquor Licenses Commissioner Peccole moved the following Resolution be adopted:

WHEREAS, Ordinance No. 447 of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada, limits
the issuance of certain Liquor Licenses to one license per 1,000 population;
and

WHEREAS, the official United States census for the year 1950 discloses
the population of the City of Las Vegas to be approximately 25,000 people; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners has determined that the official census
has not kept pace with the present population of the City of Las Vegas; and

WHEREAS, the City Manager has submitted an estimated population for the City
of Las Vegas wherein the figure is set at approximately 34,000; and

WHEREAS, for the purpose of granting liquor licenses, the population of the
City of Las Vegas, within the meaning and restrictions of Ordinance No. 447 should
be determined to be 34,000,

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the Board of Commissioners at a regular
meeting thereof, held on December 30, 1952, that the population of the City of Las
Vegas, for the purpose of granting liquor licenses, as of January 1, 1953, is hereby
let at 34,000.
