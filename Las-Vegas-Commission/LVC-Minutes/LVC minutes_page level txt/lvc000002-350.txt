OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK OF THE CITY OF
LAS VEGAS, CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

October 17th, 1938.

At a recessed regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of
Las Vegas, Clark County, Nevada, held on the 17th day of October, 1928, at the hour of
three o'clock, M.

PRESENT:	His Honor, the Mayor, J. F. Hesse, Commissioners O. J. Smith, W. C. German,

W. B. Mundy, Roy Neagle, together with the City Attorney and Clerk.

Meeting called to order by the Mayor.

At this time the Board proceeded to open and consider bids received for the purchase
of $12,000.00 Paving. Bonds to be issued by the City of Las Vegas. Thereupon the Clerk
opened the sealed bids of the First State Bank, Inc., of Las Vegas, Nevada, and of the
firm of Snow, Goodart & Company, Salt Lake City, said letters reading as follows:-

"		Las Vegas, Nevada

October 17, 1928.

The Board of City Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas,

City.

Gentlemen:

We hereby bid for your $12,000.00 Las Vegas Special 1928 Pavement and Curb Improvement
Bonds, bearing 5% interest, payable semi annually, the par value thereof, plus accrued
interest. This bid is subject to the approval of our Attorney as to the legality of the
issue.

City to furnish the bonds.

Enclosed find Cashier's Check in favor of the City Clerk for $600.00, being 5% of the
amount bid.

Respectfully submitted.

FIRST STATE BANK
By C. S. Wengert

W/H	CASHIER.		"

"	Salt Lake City

October _____, 1928.

Honorable Mayor and City Council
Las Vegas, Nevada.

Gentlemen:

For $12,000 Paving Bonds of the City of Las Vegas, to be issued in accordance with your
Official Notice of Sale, we are pleased to make you the following offers:

1.	For bonds bearing 6% interest, we will pay you 100 and accrued interest, plus a
premium of $260.00 on the entire issue.

2.	For bonds bearing 5 1/2% interest, we will pay you 100 and accrued interest, plus a
premium of $22.00 on the entire issue.

It is understood and agreed that you will take such steps and pass such resolutions
and execute such papers as our Attorneys may require to the end that said bonds may be
legally issued and delivered to us without undue or unnecessary delay.

Inclosed is our Cashier's Check, for $650.00, as evidence of our good faith. This check
is to be returned to our representative in case our bid is not accepted and is to be held
by you uncashed in case our bid is accepted and applied as part payment on the bonds at the
time of their delivery to us.

Respectfully submitted,

SNOW, GOODART & COMPANY

By Gordon Snow_________

The above offer No. _____is hereby accepted for and on behalf of the City of Las Vegas.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, Witness our hands and the seal of said City, this 6_____ day of October,

A. D. 1928.
