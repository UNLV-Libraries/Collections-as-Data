Las Vegas, Nevada
May 21, 1952

A regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas held this 21st
day of May, 1952 was called to order at the hour of 7:35 P.M. by His Honor Mayor C.D. Baker
with the following members present:

Mayor	C.D. Baker

Commissioner Wendell Bunker
Commissioner Rex A. Jarrett
Commissioner William Peccole
Commissioner Reed Whipple

Assistant

City Manager Dora Lord

City Attorney Howard W. Cannon

City Clerk Shirley Ballinger

Absent:	City Manager C.W. Shelley

Commissioner Bunker moved that Payroll Warrants Nos. 1299 to 1303 inclusive in the net amount
of $30,030.27 be approved and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to sign same.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Whipple moved that Service and Material Warrants Nos. 2271 to 2274 inclusive
in the total amount of $43,659.30 be approved and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to
sign same.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved that the Minutes of May 5, 7, 9 and 12, 1952 be approved by refer-
ence and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to sign same.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Jarrett moved the Humane Society contract be renewed from June 1, 1952 to June 1,
1953 under the same terms and conditions as the previous contract, and the Mayor and City Clerk
authorized to sign same.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved that pursuant to the recommendation of the City Engineer that
Assessment District No. 100-6 be accepted subject to notification from the City Engineer
that the chips on these streets have been cleaned up.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the request of the Las Vegas PTA to hold a circus June 27 and 28,
1952 be approved providing the Police Department is contacted for policing activities and the
City is not held liable for any of the activities of this circus.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the request of the Clark County Sheriff's Relief Association to
hold a fireworks display at Cashman Field on July 3, 1952 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Whipple moved the Mayor be authorized to sign the petition for street lighting
assessment for the installation of street lights along Highway 91.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

At this time Commissioner Bunker left the meeting.
