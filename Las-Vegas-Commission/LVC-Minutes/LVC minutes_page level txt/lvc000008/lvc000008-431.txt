WHEREAS, on June 11, 1953, the Regional Planning Commission approved the application
inasmuch as they felt that there is adequate space to provide for off street parking, the
plans of the church show it to be an attractive structure and the Commission did not feel
that a church in this location would be detrimental to property values in the area; and

WHEREAS, such decision was filed with the Board of Commissioners on June 15, 1953,
recommending that such Use Permit be granted.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the recommendation of the Regional Planning Commission
that the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints be granted a Use Permit to construct
a church in a portion of the SW¼ SW¼ SW¼ Section 32, T20S, R61E, MDB&M., on a lot which
is 233 feet by 273 feet and is generally located on the north side of Charleston Boulevard
and west of Barnard Drive be and the same hereby is approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Whipple.

At this time the Board considered the recommendation of the Regional Planning Commission
that the application of the Seventy-Day Adventist Church to conduct a series of religious
revival services on the northwest corner of Wilson and D Streets be approved.

Commissioner Bunker moved the recommendation of the Planning Commission be approved and
the following resolution adopted:

WHEREAS, on the 9th day of June, 1953, Pastor H. W. Kibble, Jr., of the Seventh-Day
Adventist Church, filed an application with the Regional Planning Commission for Use Per-
mit to conduct a series of religious revival services beginning July 1, 1953 and ending
October 1, 1953 on the northwest corner of Wilson and "D" Streets in a fire resistant tent
which size if 1+0 feet by 50 feet; and

WHEREAS, on June 11, 1953, the Regional Planning Commission held a public hearing on
this application for Use Permit; and

WHEREAS, on June 11, 1953, the Regional Planning Commission voted to recommend that
this application for Use Permit be approved subject to adequate parking being made avail-
able, and to fire and health department regulations; and

WHEREAS, such decision was filed with the Board of Commissioners on June 15, 1953,
recommending that such Use Permit be approved.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the recommendation of the Regional PLanning Commission
that the application of Pastor H. W. Kibble, Jr., of the Seventh-Day Adventist Church to
conduct a series of religious revival services beginning July 1, 1953 and ending October 1,
1953 on the northwest comer of Wilson and "D" Streets in a fire resistant tent which size
is 40 feet by 50 feet be approved subject to adequate parking being made available, and to
fire and health department regulations, be and the same hereby is accepted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner Whipple.

Commissioner Bunker, member of the committee to make recommendation on the proposed
Speeding ordinance requested additional time before reporting.

An ordinance entitled: AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND CHAPTER 36, SECTION 42, CODE OF LAS VEGAS,
NEVADA, 1949, MAKING IT UNLAWFUL FOR ANY PERSON TO DRIVE A MOTOR VEHICLE WHILE INTOXICATED
OR UNDER THE INFLUENCE OF INTOXICATING LIQUORS, OR STIMULATING OR STUPEFYING DRUGS; PROVID-
ING FOR FINES AND PENALTIES UPON CONVICTION; PROVIDING FOR OTHER MATTERS PROPERLY RELATED
THERETO; AND REPEALING ALL ORDINANCES AND PARTS OF ORDINANCES IN CONFLICT HEREWITH, was
read by title and referred to Commissioners Bunker and Whipple.

It was the determination of the Board that the proposed Speeding Ordinance and the above
entitled Drunk Drivers Ordinance should be incorporated in one ordinance.

Commissioners Jarrett and Sharp requested additional time before reporting on the proposed
ordinance regulating Street Photography.
