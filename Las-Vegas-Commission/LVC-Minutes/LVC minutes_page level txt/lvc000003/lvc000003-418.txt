NOVEMBER 17th, 1935

 Minutes of a Special Called meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas,
lark County, Nevada, held on the 16th day of November, 1935 at the hour of eleven o'clock A.M.

The following answered roll call, Mayor Arnett, Commissioners Down, Farnsworth, Marble and
Ward, and City Attorney Taylor and City Clerk Viola Burns.

The purpose of the meeting, the considering of the purchasing of land to be used as a
municipal air port, being stated the Board at once entered into a discussion on the matter
and after due consideration on the matter the following Preambles and Resolution were duly
adopted, on motion of Commissioner Ward, seconded by Commissioner Farnsworth:

		

WHEREAS, it appears to the Board of City Commissioners that a municipal airport for the City
of Las Vegas would be of public use and purpose and of great convenience and benefit to the
City of Las Vegas as well as a great advantage to the surrounding community, and

WHEREAS, the engineers of the Transcontinental & Western Air, INc. have approved the
hereinafter described land as being suitable and adequate for an airport, and

WHEREAS, it appears that the Federal Government has offered to expend upon such airport
in the event the City procures the land for such purpose, a sum amounting to approximately
One Hundred Thousand ($100,000) Dollars, now therefore, be it

RESOLVED: That L.L. Arnett, Mayor of the City of Las Vegas, is hereby authorized directed
and empowered to enter into an agreement for an on behalf of the City and to bind the City for
the purchase of and he is hereby authorized and directed to purchase for the use and benefit
of the City the following described land:

All the east half (E½) and all the north half of southwest quarter (N½SW¼): and all of
the southeast quarter of southwest quarter (SE¼ SW¼) Sec. 36, Township 20 South, Range 61 East.,

M.	D.B.M., Clark County, containing 440 acres more or less, according to the government survey
there of.
