At this time the resignation of Lloyd S. Payne, City Electrical inspector for sometime
past was presented to the Board for consideration. Same to be effective as of December 15th, 1934

On motion of Commissioner Down seconded by Commissioner Arnett it was moved and carried
that the resignation of Lloyd S. Payne be accepted, and that A.L. Drew, present Supt of the
Garbage Dept. be appointed Electrical Inspector to succeed Mr. Payne and that S.C. Scott be
appointed to take Mr. Drew's place as Supt. of Garbage Collection Dept. for the City of Las
Vegas. Appointments to be effective as of December 17th, 1934.

Vote was as follows: Commissioners Arnett, Marble, German and Down and His Honor the
Mayor voting aye. Noes none.

An ordinance entitled, "AN ORDINANCE TO PROHIBIT THE DRINKING OF INTOXICATING LIQUORS
IN ANY PUBLIC PLACE OR IN VEHICLES IN ANY PUBLIC PLACE IN THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS, NEVADA, AND
PROVIDING A PUNISHMENT THEREFOR," was read for a second time and passed by the following vote:
Commissioners Arnett, Marble, German and Down and His Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes none.

Said Ordinance to be known as Ordinance No. 212, and to be in full force and effect from this
day forward.

An Ordinance entitled, "AN ORDINANCE FORBIDDING SOLICITORS, PEDDLERS, HAWKERS. ITINERANT
MERCHANTS AND TRANSIENT VENDORS OF MERCHANDISE FROM TRESPASSING IN AND UPON PRIVATE RESIDENCES
WHEN UNINVITED SO TO DO BY THE OWNERS AND OCCUPANTS OF SAID PRIVATE RESIDENCES. IN SOLICITING
AND ATTEMPTING TO SOLICIT ORDERS, OR IN SELLING AND EXPOSING FOR SALE WARES AND MERCHANDISE,

AND DECLARING SUCH SOLICITING TO BE A NUISANCE, AND FIXING THE PENALTY FOR COMMITTING SUCH
NUISANCES," was read for a second time and passed by the following vote; Commissioners Arnett,

Said Ordinance to be known as Ordinance No 213 of the City of Las Vegas and to be in
full force and effect from and after this reading.

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK. CITY OF LAS VEGAS.
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA,

DECEMBER 13th, 1934.

Minutes of a recessed regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las
Vegas, Nevada, held on the 13th day of December, 1934 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

Following answered roll call. Mayor Cragin, Commissioners Arnett, Marble, and Down,

City Attorney and City Clerk.

Absent Commissioner German.

At this time Walter Hunsaker appeared before the Board in regard to securing permission
from the Board of City Commissioners for the erection of a permanent portico over a portion of
the sidewalk on the north east corner of Fremont and Third Sts. Said portico to be a part of
the Rio Grande Oil Company Service station to be erected on the said site.

After some discussion on the matter on motion of Commissioner Arnett seconded by Commiss-
ioner Down it was moved and carried that the matter be referred to the City Planning Commission
for recommendation.

Vote was as follows: Commissioners Arnett, Marble and Down and His Honor the Mayor voting
aye. Noes none.

An Ordinance entitled, "An Ordinance regulating the making, carrying, keeping and selling
of certain weapons and devices," was read for a first time and adopted by the following vote:
Commissioners Arnett, Marble and Down and His Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes none.

he City Clerk was directed to have the above mentioned ordinance published in the Las
Vegas Age for a period of two weeks, that is two times as is provided by law. Said Ordinance to
be known as Ordinance No. 214.

No further business appearing before the Board at this time the meeting recessed until
Friday the 28th day of December, 1934 at the hour of three o'Clock P.M.
