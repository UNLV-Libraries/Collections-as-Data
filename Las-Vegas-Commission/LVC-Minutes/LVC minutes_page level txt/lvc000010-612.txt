This being the time heretofore set for a public meeting on the proposed
lease of property to Clark County for the Clark County Fair & Recreation
Board for the construction of swimming pools, Mayor Baker explained that
this property was located at the John C. Fremont School and Hyde Park areas.

His Honor asked if there were any questions or objections to this lease of
property. None were voiced or filed.

Thereafter Commissioner Sharp moved the following resolution be adopted:

	RESOLUTION

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas has held
a public hearing on the desirability of leasing certain real property owned
by the City of Las Vegas to the County of Clark for use by the Clark County
Fair and Recreation Board; and

WHEREAS, all the requirements of Chapter 98, Laws of Nevada, 1957, have
been complied with; and

WHEREAS, there have been no protests, either oral or written, objecting
to the City entering into a lease for ninety-nine (99) years with the County
of Clark; and

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners has determined that the said land
can be put to a greater public use by the County of Clark and the Clark
County Fair and Recreation Board, in accordance with their powers and
duties as prescribed by the law of the State of Nevada,

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS OF THE
CITY OF LAS VEGAS that the Mayor of the City of Las Vegas be and he here-
by is authorized to enter into a lease with the County of Clark for a
term of ninety-nine (99) years and in accordance with all the terms and
conditions contained in that certain agreement dated the 6th day of
November, 1957, which has been examined and approved by all of the de-
partments concerned in the City of Las Vegas.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.
Absent:	Commissioner Fountain.
