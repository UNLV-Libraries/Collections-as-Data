WHEREAS, trade and commerce within the City of Las Vegas is affected by such
property damage in that the sale in this trading area of farm products grown in
Lincoln and Clark Counties would be impeded and restricted; and

WHEREAS, traffic and communications to Las Vegas are affected by such floods
due to the damage to highways, bridges, railroad lines, and telephone lines; and

WHEREAS, the United States Congress, by Act approved May 17, 1950 (Public Law
516, 8lst Congress, 2nd Session), authorized the construction, under the direction
of the Secretary of the Army and the supervision of the Chief of Engineers,of two
dams in the Meadow Valley Wash drainage area, one in Pine Canyon, and the other in
Mathew Canyon, in Nevada; and

WHEREAS, a total of $132,000.00 has been appropriated to cover planning ex-
penditures through fiscal year 1956; and

WHEREAS, all surveys and plans for construction of Pine and Mathews Dams will
be fully completed by the end of 1956; and

WHEREAS, an addition $2,210,000.00 remains to be appropriated to complete the
project;

NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED, that the present session of the Congress be urged
to proceed with securing the necessary appropriations for construction of these
dams and make every effort to speed up completion of all structures concerned there-
with at the earliest date possible.

ADOPTED and APPROVED the 26th day of April, 1956 by the Board of Commissioners
of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada.
