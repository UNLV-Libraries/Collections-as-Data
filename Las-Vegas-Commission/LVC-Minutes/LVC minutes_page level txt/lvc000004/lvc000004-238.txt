RESOLUTION

FIXING NUMBER OF POLICEMEN, HOURS OR WORK AND SALARY OF THE MEMBERS OF THE LAS VEGAS POLICE

FORCE.

WHEREAS, the present police force of the City of Las Vegas, Clark County Nevada, consists of a
Chief of Police, one acting Chief of Police and eight Patrolmen, exclusive of one man on Mer-
chants Patrol and One Radio Technician, and

WHEREAS the patrolmen on said force and the captains are now working a shift of twelve hours each
day, end

WHEREAS it is desired to change said working day from twelve to eight hours and that by reason
thereof it will become necessary to engage two extra men to be known as Relief Patrolmen,

NOW, THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED by the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, Clark County,
Nevada, that from and after the 1st day of January, A.D. 1940, eight hours shall constitute a
work day for each member of the Police Force, which shall mean eight hours of actual duty service
and shall not include time taken for meals, and that from and after the said 1st day of January
A.D., 1940, and until the further action by this Board, the salary of the respective members of
said Police Force (not including said Merchants' Patrolman and the said Radio Technician) shall
be as follows:

Chief of Police	$225.00	per	month

Two Captains of Police, each	180.00	"	"

Seven patrolmen, each	170.00	"	"

Two Relief men, each	150.00	"	"

Police Commissioner, Corradetti moved the adoption of the above and foregoing resolution and the
same was seconded by Commissioner Krause, and upon being put to vote was duly adopted, at a regular
(adjourned) meeting of said Board held on the 28th day of December, A.D. 1939 by the following vote:
Voting aye: Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, Krause and Corradetti and His Honor the Mayor voting.
voting no, none.

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK CITY OF
LAS VEGAS CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

JANUARY 4th, 1940.

Minutes of a regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas,

Clark County, Nevada, held on the 4th day of January, 1940 at the hour of seven thirty P.M.

Present Mayor Russell, Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, and Krause, City Attorney and Clerk.

Absent Commissioner Corradetti.

Attorney Hinman appeared before the Board and ask that the City take the necessary steps to
deed back to him ground on which a portion of the golf course is located. Mr. Hinman claiming
that the City had not lived up to the agreement and had not fulfilled the terms of said agree-
ment.

Mr. Austin City Attorney was instructed to search the deed to this property and report
his findings to the Board in the near future.

On motion of Commissioner Ronnow seconded by Commissioner Gilbert it was moved and carried
that Warrants Nos. 276 to 285 be allowed and that the Mayor and Clerk sign the same.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, and Krause, and Hie Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes, none.

On	motion	of	Commissioner	Gilbert seconded	by	Commissioner	Krause	it	was	moved	and	carried

that the	report	of	the Municipal Judge A.G. Blad	be	accepted and	filed.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, and Krause and His Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes, none.

A writ of attachment served on the City by the Justice Court was presented to the Board for
consideration at this time. Said attachment being on the wages of R.D. Pollock a City employee.

On motion of Commissioner Ronnow, seconded by Commissioner Krause it was moved and carried
that this mandate be obeyed.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, Krause and Hie Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes, none.

On	motion	of	Commissioner	Gilbert seconded	by	Commissioner	Krause	it	was	moved	and	carried

that the	twenty-one (21) retail	beverage licenses applied for at	this time	be granted.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, Krause and His Honor the Mayor voting
aye. Noes, none.

On motion of Commissioner Gilbert seconded by Commissioner Krause it was moved and carried
that the five (5) sealed package liquor licenses applied for at this time be granted. This appli-
cations being for renewal of present licenses.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Gilbert, and Krause, and His Honor the Mayor voting aye. Noes,

none.
