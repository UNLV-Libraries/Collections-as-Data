A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas held

GAMING LICENSES	Commissioner Peccole moved the licenses issued the 49er Club and the Cinnabar

49er Club &	for one "21" table each be rescinded, said action taken August 22, 1951,and

Cinnabar	that these licenses be revoked and a new vote be taken by the entire Board.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

CINNABAR	Commissioner Peccole moved the application of James Fare and Robert Testolin,

Cinnabar, 122 North 2nd Street for a license for a "21" game for the third
	quarter of 1951 be granted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Jarrett, Peccole and His Honor voting aye; noes, Commissioners
Bunker and Whipple.

49er CLUB	Commissioner Peccole moved the application of Ernest Amante and J. S. Pappas,

49er Club, 109 Fremont for a license for one "21" game for the third quarter
	of 1951 be denied.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole and His Honor voting aye; noes,

Commissioner Whipple.

RESOLUTION -	Commissioner Peccole moved the resolution limiting gaming licenses issued in

Gaming License                the City of Las Vegas be reinstated.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

There being no further business to come before the meeting at this time, the
meeting adjourned.
