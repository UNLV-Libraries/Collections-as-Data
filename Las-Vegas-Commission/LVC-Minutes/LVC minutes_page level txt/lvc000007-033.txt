A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners held this 5th day of January, 1950
was called to order at the hour of 4:00 P.M. by His Honor Mayor E. W. Cragin with the
following members present: Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple; City Manager

George O. Treem, City Attorney Howard W. Cannon and City Clerk Shirley Ballinger.

Commissioner Moore moved that the application of Ruth Cannon, Richard Mozzer and David
Kapial, All Nite Market, 921 South Main for a Retail Beverage license be denied and
the Chief of Police be instructed to notify the All Nite Market that this license has
not been renewed.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Moore and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye/ noes, none*

Commissioner Moore moved that the application of John E. Smith, R. A. Gagnon, Anthony
Magrini, Keno Enterprises, 18 Fremont to conduct a Race Horse Keno be denied.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

There being no further business to come before the meeting at this time Commissioner
Moore moved this meeting adjourn until January 6, 1950 at the hour of 3:00 P.M.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.
