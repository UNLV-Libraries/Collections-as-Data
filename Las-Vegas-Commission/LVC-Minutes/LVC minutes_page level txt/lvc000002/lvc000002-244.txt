OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS,

CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA. November 27th, 1926

At a regular adjourned meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of Las
Vegas, Clark County, Nevada, held on the 24th day of November, A.D. 1926, at the hour of
three o'clock P.M. Present Hon. J.F. Hesse, Mayor, with commissioners O.J. Smith and W.B.
Mundy, together with the City Attorney, F.A. Stevens, Esq., and the city Clerk, Commissioners
C.J. Hammond and W.H. Elwell being absent.

All bills allowed as per claim book on file in the office of the Clerk of the Board.

The petition of Thomas B. Keeler for permission to lay a pipe line down the alley from
Mesquite to Linden Streets, between Seventh and Eighth Streets was granted and the City
Attorney was authorized to draw up said permit and the Mayor and City Clerk were directed
to sign said permit.

Thereupon the Board took under advisement the question of making certain improvements
to the city streets by applications of calcium chloride solution, and after a general
discussion of the matter the Street Commissioner was authorized to order one ear of the
solution and apply it as an experiment.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time it was ordered
upon motion of Commissioner Mundy, seconded by Commissioner Smith that the Board adjourn
until the next regular meeting to be held on Thursday the 2nd day of December, A.D 1926.,
at the hour of three o'clock P.M.
