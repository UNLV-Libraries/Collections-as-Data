EMERGENCY ORDINANCE NO. 317

AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND ORDINANCE NO. 292 entitled, "AN ORDINANCE PROVIDING FOR THE PROTECTION
OF THE PUBLIC HEALTH, AND THE EXAMINATION, REGISTRATION, LICENSING AND BONDING OF PERSONS
ENGAGED IN THE BUSINESS OF PLUMBING, OR LABORING AT THE TRADE OF PLUMBING; REQUIRING A
PERMIT FOR THE INSTALLATION OR ALTERATION OF PLUMBING AND DRAINAGE SYSTEMS; CREATING THE
OFFICE OF PLUMBING INSPECTOR AND A BOARD OF PLUMBER EXAMINERS, AND PRESCRIBING THEIR DUTIES;
DEFINING CERTAIN TERMS; ESTABLISHING MINIMUM REGULATIONS FOR THE INSTALLATION, ALTERATION OR
REPAIR OF PLUMBING AND DRAINAGE SYSTEMS AND THE INSPECTION THEREOF; PROVIDING PENALTIES
FOR ITS VIOLATION AND REPEALING CONFLICTING ORDINANCES."

The Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas do ordain, as follows:

Section 1. Section 1 of the above entitled Ordinance is hereby amended by amending
subdivision (a) of Section 137 of the Plumbing Code of the City of Las Vegas to read as
follows:

Sec. 127. HOUSE DRAIN AND HOUSE SEWER MATERIALS.

(a) The House Sewer, beginning three (3) feet outside the building line, shall be of
case iron pipe, vitrified clay pipe of approved standards, or pipe meeting the standards of
the Orangeburg pipe, except that such pipe meeting the standards of the Orangeburg pipe
shall not be installed beneath concrete slabs of any description, and provided that if such
pipe meeting the standards of Orangeburg pipe is used, it shall be installed according to
United States Government specifications or specifications supplied by the Plumbing Inspector
of the City of Las Vegas.

Section 2. All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict with the provisions
of this ordinance are hereby repealed.

Section 3. This ordinance is declared to be and is hereby passed as an Emergency
Ordinance by reason of the emergency conditions existing in the need for Veteran housing
and it shall take effect immediately after its passage and publication.

Section 4. The City Clerk is hereby directed to cause this Ordinance to be published
in the Las Vegas Evening Review Journal a newspaper published in the City of Las Vegas,
County of Clark, State of Nevada, for a period of two weeks, that is to say once each week
for a period of two weeks.
