Commissioner Elwell moved the matter of removing parking on Fremont Street
from 4:30 A.M. to 5:00 A.M. each, for street cleaning purposes, he held in
abeyance.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Fountain and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

Public Works Director Sauer requested permission to go to bid for the
supplying of material and fabrication of standard pipes for the Rehabilitation
Farm Irrigation System.

No action was taken with regard to this; it was decided to endeavor to
secure secondhand pipe instead.

Commissioner Fountain moved the Department of Public Works be given permission
to enter into an agreement with the Nevada State Highway Department for the
installation of conduits in conjunction with the second barrel of the
Charleston Underpass for the purpose of installing proposed future traffic
signals at Second and "A" Streets on Charleston.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Sharp moved the claim of Nathan Levine for automobile damage
in the amount of $58.50 be denied and referred to the Insurance Agency, as
recommended by the City Attorney.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Elwell and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Sharp moved the claim of Raimundo Cruz for automobile damage
in the amount of approximately $300.00 be denied and referred to the
Insurance Agency, as recommended by the City Attorney.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Sharp moved the claim of Claire Naples for injuries sustained
while skating at the Convention Center in the present amount of $25.00 and
the anticipated future amount of $10.00 be denied, and referred to the
Insurance Agency, as recommended by the City Attorney.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

City Manager Kennedy presented the request of the Sports Car Club of
America for renewal of their present lease for an additional term of one
year at an annual rental of $1.00.

Commissioner Whipple moved the lease with Sports Car Club of America for
property located on N. 2nd Street, between W. Bonanza Road and the American
Legion, be renewed for one year at an annual rental of $1.00.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Elwell and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
voting aye; noes, none.

City Manager Kennedy again presented the request of the Nevada Club to hold
a fishing contest in 1960 and thereafter, which had been held in abeyance
at the last meeting. It was decided no action would be taken on this
matter until the Resolution on Give-Aways had been adopted.

City Manager Kennedy presented a letter from Judge Zenoff, requesting that
His Honor, Mayor Gragson, or one of the Commissioners, be appointed to
the Board of the Spring Mountain Youth Camp, to act in an advisory capacity.

His Honor, Mayor Gragson, stated he would like to appoint Commissioner
Fountain.

Commissioner Whipple moved the recommendation of His Honor, Mayor Gragson,
be approved and Commissioner Fountain be appointed to the Board of the
Spring Mountain Youth Camp.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Elwell, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor, Mayor Gragson, voting
aye; noes, none. Commissioner Fountain passed his vote.

City Manager Kennedy advised the General Fund Balance as of January 15, 1960
was $870,916.85; Estimated Gross Payroll was $113,000.00, and Service &
Material Warrants were $138,504.77.
