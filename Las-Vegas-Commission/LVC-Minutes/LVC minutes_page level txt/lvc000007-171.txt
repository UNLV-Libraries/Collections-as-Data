Commissioner Moore moved that pursuant to the favorable recommendation
of the Police Department the application of Edward R. Moss, Goldfield
Club, 114-116 Fremont for a Retail Tavern Liquor license for the third
quarter of 1950 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the petition heretofore received on July 7,
1950 from property owners in the two hundred block on South 10th Street
be referred to the Regional Planning Commission for study to see if
preventive measures can be taken in this situation.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the request of the Boy Scouts of America to
use the Auditorium all day of October 7, 1950 without charge, be allowed.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Moore and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

Commissioner Moore moved the City Manager be instructed to put on a man
for at least fifteen days on the Westside to work in conjunction with
the Poundmaster to clear up the situation of so many dogs running at
large.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

There being no further business to come before the meeting at this time
Commissioner Moore moved this meeting adjourn until September 22, 1950
at the hour of 3:00 P.M.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.
