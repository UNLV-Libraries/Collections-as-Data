Commissioner Whipple moved that the purchase of furniture for the City Manager's
and the City Attorney's office be authorized.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

At this time the City Manager presented the request of the YMCA to use the Wildcat
Lair during the construction of their own building.

This request vas referred to the City Manager to find out whether they would be
willing to use the building on a month to month basis.

Mr. E. V. Martinet, president of the Las Vegas Lions Club appeared before the Board
and stated that his organization would like to contribute toward equipment for some
city park.

Discussion was held and the City Manager was requested to write a letter to the
Las Vegas Lions Club, suggesting three parks which might be benefited by such a
contribution.

At this time the agreement between the Southern Nevada Telephone Company and the
City Fire Department on the fire alarm system was discussed.

No action was taken by the Board at this time.

An emergency ordinance entitled: AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND ORDINANCE NO. 364 ALSO KNOWN
AS CHAPTER 29, CODE OF LAS VEGAS, NEVADA, 1949, BY PROVIDING A NEW SCHEDULE OF RATES
FOR SERVICE RENDERED BY AND THROUGH THE MUNICIPAL SEWAGE DISPOSAL SYSTEM; PROVIDING
OTHER MATTERS RELATING THERETO; AND REPEALING ALL ORDINANCES OR PARTS OF ORDINANCES
IN CONFLICT THEREWITH, was referred to Commissioners Sharp and Bunker for further
report.

The City Manager requested permission to purchase 30 or 40 tickets to enable city
employees to attend the Sales Executive Meeting to be held February 10, 1955.
Permission was granted for this expenditure.

At this time Mr. George Wade, City Engineer, requested the purchase of 100 chairs
for the City Council Chambers. He said that these chairs, a sample of which was
exhibited, would cost approximately $13.00 plus $1.25 each for installation and
freight costs from Los Angeles.

Commissioner Whipple moved that this purchase be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

City Manager George Rounthwaite stated that the fire department needed to purchase
100 new fire hydrants for $11,250.00 out of the revolving fund, and recommended
the Board's approval of this purchase.

Commissioner Whipple moved the purchase of these fire hydrants for $11,250.00 be
approved by the Board.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

An ordinance entitled: AN ORDINANCE TO AMEND ORDINANCE NO. 70, ALSO KNOWN AS CHAPTER
21, SECTION 54, CODE OF LAS VEGAS, NEVADA, 1949; BY DEFINING THE OFFENSE OF VAGRANCY;
PROVIDING PUNISHMENT UPON CONVICTION THEREFORE; PROVIDING OTHER MATTERS RELATED
THERETO; AND REPEALING ALL ORDINANCES OR PARTS OF ORDINANCES IN CONFLICT THEREWITH,
having been reported on favorably by Commissioners Jarrett and Sharp, was read in
full to the Board.

Thereafter Commissioner Jarrett moved the above entitled ordinance No. 631 be
adopted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.

An ordinance entitled: AN EMERGENCY ORDINANCE TO AMEND THE PLANNING AND ZONING
ORDINANCE, ALSO KNOWN AS CHAPTER 24, CODE OF LAS VEGAS, NEVADA, 1949, BY ADDING
TWO NEW CLASSES OF DISTRICTS, TO ALLOW AND PERMIT THE ESTABLISHMENT OF TRAILER
PARKS UPON MEETING CERTAIN CONDITIONS; PROVIDING OTHER MATTERS PROPERLY RELATED
THERETO; AND REPEALING ALL ORDINANCES OR PARTS OF ORDINANCES IN CONFLICT
THEREWITH was read in full to the Board.

Thereafter Commissioner Jarrett moved the above entitled ordinance No. 630. be adopted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent: Commissioner
Bunker.
