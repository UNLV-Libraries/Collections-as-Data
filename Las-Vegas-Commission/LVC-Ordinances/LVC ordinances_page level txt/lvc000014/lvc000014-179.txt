EMERGENCY ORDINANCE

ORDINANCE NO. 270

AN ORDINANCE CREATING A PLANNING COMMISSION FOR THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS, DEFINING THE POWERS
AND DUTIES THEREOF AND MATTERS RELATING THERETO.

The Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, do ordain as follows:

SECTION: 1. It is hereby created a planning commission for the City of Las Vegas in accord-
ance with Chapter 110, Statutes of Nevada, 1941, approved March 28, 1941, consisting of
nine members. Six members shall be appointed by the Mayor of the City of Las Vegas with
the approval of the City Commissioners; the remaining three members shall consist of the
City Engineer of the City of Las Vegas, the Mayor of the City of Las Vegas and the City
Clerk. The City Engineer, the Mayor and the City Clerk shall be ex-officio members. The
term of each appointed member shall be six years, or until his successor takes office,
except that the respective terms of five of the members first appointed shall be one, two,
three, four and five years. Members may be relieved after public hearing by a majority
vote of the governing body for inefficiency, neglect of duty or malfeasance of office. A
vacancy occurring otherwise than through the expiration of term shall be filled for the
unexpired term by the Mayor of the City of Las Vegas with the approval of the Board of
Commissioners.

SECTION 2. The said planning commission shall have the powers and duties and be governed
by the proceedings prescribed by Chapter 110, Statutes of Nevada, 1941, approved March 28,

1941.

SECTION 3. This ordinance is declared to be and is hereby passed as an EMERGENCY ORDINANCE
and it shall take effect immediately after its passage and publication.

SECTION 4. The City Clerk is hereby authorized to have this Ordinance published in the
Las Vegas Review Journal, a daily newspaper, printed and published in the City of Las Vegas
County of Clark, State of Nevada, for a period of two weeks, that is to say once each week
for a period of two weeks.

The foregoing Ordinance was read aloud for the first time and voted upon at a regular meet-
ing of the Board of City Commissioners on the 6th day of April, 1942.

Vote for: Commissioners: Smith, Tinch, Clark, Rubidoux, and his Honor the Mayor, Howell C.

Garrison, and was read aloud for the second time and passed by the unanimous consent of the
