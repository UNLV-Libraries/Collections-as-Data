At this time Mrs. Peggy Petrie presented resolutions to the
Board from the AAUW and Women's Society of Christian Service,

First Methodist Church, requesting suitable sewage System
in the Zaugg Tract, water lines in said tract, and that an
assistant Building Inspector of the Negro race be employed
for this specific district.

His Honor told Mrs. Petrie that this matter would be taken under
consideration.

Commissioner Whipple moved the application of George H. Schwartz
for a license for the retail sale of cigarettes and tobacco
products at the H & B All Nite Market, 1619 Fremont be granted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His
Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the application of Chester S. Biele,

Dairy Bar, 1525 Fremont for license for one additional slot machine
for the second quarter of 1951 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His

Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Whipple moved the application of L. L. Brown, H & B
All Nite Market, 1619 Fremont for a Retail Beverage Liquor License
for the second quarter of 1951 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His

Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved the final map as amended of Beverly
Green Tract No. 2 be approved.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.

At this time the request of the Southern Nevada Power Company to
install additional transformers at the San Francisco Street Sub-station
was presented to the Board for their consideration.

Mr. George Rudiak, Representing Dr. Levon Kassabian and his brother
was present protesting the installation of these transformer.

Mr. L. H. Compton of the Southern Nevada Power Company was also
present to answer questions and request the approval of the Board.

Thereafter, Commissioner Bunker moved this matter be referred to the
City Attorney for an opinion as to the legality of the action taken
by the Planning Commission.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Bunker moved the resignation of A. C. Grant from the
Civil Service Board be accepted.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Peccole moved that Ralph H. Mohr be appointed to fill
the vacancy on the Civil Service Board created by the resignation
of Mr. Grant.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Bunker moved that A. C. Grant and H. E. Hazard be appointed
as commissioners of the Housing Authority of the City of Las Vegas
for a four year term.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Bunker moved that Commissioner Whipple be appointed as
Mayor Pro Tem.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Peccole and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor
voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Bunker moved the next regular meetings be held on
Wednesday June 13 and 20, 1951 at 3:30 P.M. and after the scheduled
meetings have been held the regular meetings of the Board be held
the first and third Wednesday of each month at 7:30 P.M.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following
vote: Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor

voting aye; noes, none.
