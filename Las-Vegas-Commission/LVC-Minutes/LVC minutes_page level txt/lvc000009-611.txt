Commissioner Sharp moved the following resolution directing that a public hearing
on the creation of Assessment District No. 100-36 be held September 21, 1955, be
adopted:

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, have taken
steps to create certain public improvements all as provided in Ordinance No. 661,
passed, adopted and approved this 8th day of September, 1955,

WHEREAS, said Ordinance No. 661 discloses the intention and determination
of said Board of Commissioners to create said district and construct said improvements
by special assessment made according to benefits; and

WHEREAS, said Ordinance No. 661 provides a time certain for hearing pro-
tests against said proposed improvements and directing that notice thereof be given,
such time of the protest hearing being 8:00 P.M., on the 21st day of September,

1955,

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas at a recessed regular meeting thereof held on the 8th day of September,
1955, that the City Clerk and Clerk of the Board of Commissioners be and she is
hereby directed to publish notice of said hearing once a week for two successive
weeks in the Las Vegas Review Journal, a daily newspaper published in the City of
Las Vegas; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the City Clerk and Clerk of the Board of
Commissioners be and she hereby is directed to post notice of said hearing in at
least three public places near the site of the proposed work; and

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that said notice shall be in substantially the
form attached to this Resolution and made a part hereof.

ADOPTED AND APPROVED this 8th day of September, 1955.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Fountain and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.

A. H. Kennedy, City Manager, presented reports from the License Department and the

been allowing juveniles to play slot machines, and requested that the licensee be
instructed to appear before the Board to show cause why the license should not be
revoked.

Thereafter Commissioner Sharp moved that W. Howard Adams, licensee of the Eldorado
Arcade, 4 Fremont Street, be instructed to appear before the Commission at the
next regular meeting on September 21, 1955 to show cause why his license should
not be revoked.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Fountain and carried by the following vote:
