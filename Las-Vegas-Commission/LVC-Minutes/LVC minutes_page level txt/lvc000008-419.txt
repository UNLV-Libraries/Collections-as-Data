VARIANCE APPLICATIONS
Public Hearing
Howard Burgess

This being the time set for the public hearing on the denial of the application of Howard Bur-
gess for variances to permit the following uses:

1)	Construct and maintain a dormitory for men at 213 North 2nd Street.

2)	Construct and maintain a trailer park north of Bonanza Road, south of Marble Manor and

His Honor asked if there was anyone present who wished to protest these applications for vari-
ance.

Mrs. Alice Buntin, Mr. W.W. Cantrell and Mrs. Mary Nettles voiced their objections to the constru-
ction of a dormitory at 213 North 2nd Street.

Howard W. Cannon, property owner in this area, stated that he was not opposed to the construction
of a dormitory at this location.

Thereafter the matter was referred to Commissioners Bunker and Jarrett for investigation and re-
commendation.

Commissioner Bunker moved the applications of Howard Burgess to construct and maintain a dormi-
tory for men at 213 North 2nd and a trailer park north of Bonanza Road, south of Marble Manor and
west of H Street be deferred until June 17, 1953.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners Bunker,

Jarrett, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent:	Commissioner Peccole.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time, the meeting adjourned.
