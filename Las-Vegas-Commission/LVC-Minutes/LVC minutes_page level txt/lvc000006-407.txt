Las Vegas, Nevada
April 7, 1949

A regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners held this 7th day of April, 1949 was called
 to order at the hour of 2:55 P.M. by His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark with the following

members present: Commissioners Baskin, Moore, Whipple; City Manager J. M. Murphy, City
Attorney C. Norman Cornwall and City Clerk Shirley Ballinger. Absent:	Mayor Cragin.

MINUTES	Commissioner Baskin moved that the minutes of March 22, 25, 30, 1949 be approved by

reference and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to sign same.

	Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

	Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark, voting aye; noes, none. Absent:

Mayor Cragin.

* PAYROLL WARRANTS	Commissioner Baskin moved that Payroll Warrants Nos. 1027 thru 1030 inc. in the net sum

of $30,097.45 be approved and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to sign same.

WARRANTS	and 1777 (partial) to 1779 inclusive and 1775 in the net sum of $17,781.82 be approved

WATER FRANCHISE	Commissioner Moore moved that the request of Ted Michelas for a water franchise in the

Ted Michelas	South Meadows addition be referred to the City Attorney.



Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark voting aye; noes, none. Absent:

Mayor Cragin.

SEALED RACE TIPS	Commissioner Whipple moved that the application of the Pioneer Newsstand for the sale of

Pioneer News	sealed race tips be denied.



Motion seconded by Commissioner Moore and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark voting aye; noes, none. Absent:
Mayor Cragin.

OPENING OF BIDS	This being the time set for the opening of bids on the sale of City owned property located

Sale of City Property on the Los Angeles Highway, there were no bids received.

ZONE EXCEPTION #68	Commissioner Baskin moved that pursuant to the recommendation of the Planning Commission

Jack Woods	dated April 5, 1949 that the request of Jack Woods for a Nursery School in Huntridge

Nursery School	Tract #1, Lot 13, Block 1 be granted subject to the conditions set forth by the Planning

Commission in their letter of the above date, with further recommendation that there be
	a limit of fifteen children allowed in the school.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Moore and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark voting aye; noes, none. Absent:
Mayor Cragin.

CHARLESTON SQ #4	Commissioner Whipple moved that the Tentative Map of Charleston Square #4 be approved	upon

Tentative Map	the recommendation of the Planning Commission as per their letter dated April 5, 1949

	subject to the conditions set forth by the Planning Commission in said letter, plus the

further recommendation of the Board of Commissioners that service drive be required on
the Charleston Boulevard frontage.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Baskin and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark voting aye; noes, none. Absent:
Mayor Cragin.

LIQUOR APPLICATIONS	At this time the following new applications for liquor licenses for the second quarter of

1949 were presented to the Board for their consideration:

	Catherine & John	Horden	The Westerner	Retail	Tavern

Thomas A. Oakey	Huntridge Theatre	Retail	Package

George Fortson	Westside Liquor Store	Sealed	Package

Thereafter Commissioner Baskin moved that the foregoing applications for new liquor licenses
be denied on the basis of the resolution adopted by the Board on June 27, 1947, limiting
liquor licenses according to the population.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Moore and carried by the following vote: Commissioners
Baskin, Moore, Whipple and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Clark voting aye; noes, none. Absent:
Mayor Cragin.

The following renewal applications for liquor licenses with change in owners were presented

Mayor Cragin.
