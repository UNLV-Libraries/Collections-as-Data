WHEREAS, the time has arrived to direct the Notice of Special Assessment
to be published as required by Section 67 of the harter of the City of Las
Vegas,

NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas, at a regular meeting thereof held on the 21st day of April, 19%
that the City Clerk and Clerk of the Board of Commissioners be and she is
hereby directed to publish notice of the time and place when and where the
Board and the County Assessor and Ex-Officio City Assessor will meet to con-
sider objections to said special assessment roll and to review said assessment.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that said Notice of Special Assessment shall be
published once a week for two successive weeks, in the Las Vegas Sun, a daily
newspaper published in the City of Las Vegas.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that said Notice of Special Assessment shall be in
substantially the form attached to this Resolution and made a part hereof.

Motion seconded by Commissioner "harp and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Whipple
voting aye; noes, none. Absent:	Mayor Baker.

Commissioner Jarrett moved the following resolution directing publication
of the Notice of Public Hearing on Assessment Roll for Assessment District
No. 100-27 be adopted:

WHEREAS, the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas have taken
steps to create certain public improvements all as provided for in Emergency
Ordinance No. 603, passed, adopted and approved the 12th day of April, 19%.

WHEREAS, Section 6 of said Emergency Ordinance No. 603 directs the County
Assessor of the County of Clark, State of Nevada, the Ex-officio City Assessor
of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada, to make an assessment roll in accordance
with the Charter of the City of Las Vegas; and

WHEREAS, the said Ex-Officio City Assessor has prepared such Assessment
Roll in accordance with said Ordinance and on the 21st day of April, 1954,
filed said Assessment Roll with City Clerk of the City of Las Vegas, all as
directed by Section 6 of said Emergency Ordinance No. 603; and

WHEREAS, said roll was numbered by the City Clerk of the City of Las
Vegas as directed by Section 8 of said Emergency Ordinance No. 603; and

WHEREAS, the time has arrived to direct the Notice of Special Assess-
ment to be published as required by Section 67 of the Charter of the City of
Las Vegas,

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas, at a regular meeting thereof held on the 21st day of April, 1954
that the City Clerk and Clerk of the Board of Commissioners be and she is
hereby directed to publish notice of the time and place when and where the
Board and the County Assessor and Ex-Officio City Assessor will meet to con-
sider objections to said special assessment roll and to review said assess-
ments.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that said Notice of Special Assessment shall be
published once a week for two successive weeks, in the Las Vegas Sun, a
daily newspaper published in the City of Las Vegas.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that said Notice of Special Assessment shall be
in substantially the form attached to this Resolution and made a part hereof.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote;
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Whipple
voting aye; noes, none. Absent:	Mayor Baker.

At this time the Board considered the proposal of the First California Company
for investment of $400,000.00 from the sewer bond fund, in Ninety-Day United
States Treasury Bills.

Commissioner Bunker moved the above proposal be approved and the Mayor and
City Clerk authorized to sign the warrant when prepared.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and His Honor Mayor Pro Tem Whipple
voting aye; noes, none. Absent:	Mayor Baker.

At this time Commissioners Bunker and Sharp members of the committee for re-
commendation on the proposed Electrical Code ordinance requested additional
time before reporting.

At this time Commissioners Jarrett and Whipple members of the committee for
recommendation on the proposed Warm Air Heating Code Ordinance reported
favorably.

Thereafter an ordinance entitled:	AN ORDINANCE PROVIDING FOR THE PROTECTION

OF THE PUBLIC HEALTH AND SAFETY, AND THE EXAMINATION, REGISTRATION, LICENSING
OF PERSONS ENGAGED IN THE BUSINESS OF WARM AIR HEATING, OR LABORING AT THE
TRADE AS A WARM AIR HEATING INSTALLER REQUIRING A PERMIT FOR THE INSTALLATION
OR ALTERATION OF WARM AIR HEATING APPLIANCES, AND WARM AIR HEATING SYSTEMS:
CREATING AN ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICE AND A BOARD OF WARM AIR HEATING EXAMINERS,

AND PRESCRIBING THEIR DUTIES: DEFINING CERTAIN TERMS, ESTABLISHING MINIMUM
REGULATIONS FOR THE INSTALLATION, ALTERATION OR REPAIR OF WARM AIR HEATING
APPLIANCES, AND WARM AIR HEATING SYSTEMS AND THE INSPECTION THEREOF: PROVIDING
PENALTIES FOR ITS VIOLATION AND REPEALING CONFLICTING ORDINANCES, was read
in full to the Board.
