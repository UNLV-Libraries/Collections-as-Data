Clerk at this time reported to the Board that the Power of Attorney of Paul Dor-
ing for the Union Indemnity Company and the certificate from the Secretary of the State of
Nevada authorizing the Union Indemnity Co. to do business in the State of Nevada for the
year 1932 had been received, and that the contract between the City of Las Vegas and the
Fuller Construction Company had been signed and set to the construction company and a copy
had also been sent to the office of Paul Doring in Los Angeles, California on the 30th day
of January 1932.

The matter of the releasing of the certified check for the amount of $7,000.00 de-
posited by the Fuller Construction Company at the time of the acceptance of that company's
bid for the sewer work was next brought before the Board for consideration.

On motion of Commissioners Thomas seconded by Commissioner German it was

RESOLVED, That the City Clerk on behalf of the City of Las Vegas endorse said certi-
fied check in the amount of $7,000.00 and forward same to the Fuller Construction Company.

Vote on said motion was as follows: Commissioners Mundy, German, Hansell and Thomas
and His Honor the Mayor voting Aye, Noes none.

The claim of J.E. Leaman for the sum of One Hundred Fifty Dollars for the audit of
the City books for the, third and fourth quarters of the 1931 was laid over until the further
order of the Board.

On motion of Commissioner Thomas seconded by Commissioner German it was moved that
the amount alotted to the support of the Las Vegas Library be raised from the sum of Ninety
Dollars to the sum of One Hundred Dollars per month. The same to be in effect for the month
ending February, 1932.

At this time a number of citizens of North LAs Vegas appeared before the Board of
Commissioners asking that Ordinance No. 179 of the City of Las Vegas be repealed. After
due consideration of the matter and ordinance entitled ,"An Ordinance to amend, revise and
re-enact Sections Nos. 2 and 10 of Ordinance No. 111 of the City of Las Vegas, entitled,

"An Ordinance prohibiting the manufacture, storage, or keeping, sale, offering or exposing
for sale or barter, or other disposal of intoxicating liquors in the City of Las Vegas, re-
gulating other matters pertaining thereto; providing a penalty for the violation of this
Ordinance, and repealing all ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict therewith,"
approved May 5, 1925, as amended by Ordinance No. 179; and to repeal all ordinances and
parts of ordinances in conflict therewith," was read for a second time. Said ordinances had
been read for a first time the 4th day of December, 1931.

The vote on said ordinance was as follows: Commissioners Thomas, and Hansell and
His Honor the Mayor E.W.Cragin voting Aye. Commissioners Mundy and German voting no.

No further business appearing before the Board at this time the meeting recessed
until Friday the 12th day of February 1332 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.
