Las Vegas, Nevada
October 22, 1948

A regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners held t is 22nd day of October, 1948 was
called to order At the hour of 2:15 P.M. by his Honor Mayor E. W. Cragin with the following
members present:	Commissioners Baskin, Moore, and Whipple. Absent:	Commissioner Clark. Also

present:	City Manager J. M. Murphy, City Attorney C. Norman Cornwall and City Clerk Shirley

	Ballinger.

PROPOSED ORDINANCE At this time there appeared before the Board numerous people interested in the proposed ordinance

Solicitation of	relative	to the solicitation of	funds in the	City of Las Vegas.	His Honor Mayor Cragin stated that

Funds	the said	proposed ordinance was	submitted to	committee at the last regular meeting. It is law

	that proposed ordinances be referred to committee, who in turn report favorably or unfavorably.

Since Commissioner Clark, member of the committee, is absent and Commissioner Baskin is unable
to report, the Board will take no action at this time. However, interested parties will be
notified of an open hearing to be held, at which time a committee will be appointed among business-
	men and those protesting against the proposed ordinance to work	out an applicable ordinance.

	Reverend	Hayden appeared before	the Board at	this time relative	to the City purchasing certain

property on the Westside for play ground purposes. His Honor asked Reverend Hayden to make an
appointment with City Manager J. M. Murphy and discuss the matter with him.

His Honor read a letter from Mr. John Freeman and Mr. C, w. Davies dated October 22, 1948, relative
Bus stops	to the placing of benches at bus stops. After considerable discussion Commissioner Baskin moved

	that benches be allowed at bus stops on Fremont east of 5th Street. Further that this matter

be referred to the City Manager for handling.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners Baskin,

Moore, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none. Absent:	Commissioner Clark.

Commissioner Baskin moved that the minutes of September 30, October 5, and October 7, 1948 be approved

	by reference and the Mayor and City Clerk authorized to sign same.
