Las Vegas, Nevada

CALL OF SPECIAL MEETING

TO: SHIRLEY BALLINGER, City Clerk
Las Vegas, Nevada

A Special Meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas is
hereby called to be held in the Council Chambers at the City Hall, on the
2nd day of October 1950. at the hour of 3:00 P.M. to consider the following
matters:

1.	Proposed rezoning of Govt Lot 2, Section 2 T 21 S, R 61 E from R-1 to R-4.

2.	Request of State Planning Commission for location for State Building
in City Park

3.	Acquisition of Nellis Air Force Base by Government

4.	New Liquor applications for the 4th Quarter of 1950.

s/ E.W. Cragin_____________

Mayor

A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners held this 2nd day of October,

1950 was called to order by His Honor Mayor E.W. Cragin at the hour of 3:15
P.M. with the following members present: Commissioners Bunker, Moore,

Peccole, Whipple; City Manager C.W. Shelley, City Attorney Howard W. Cannon
and City Clerk Shirley Ballinger.

PROPOSED REZONING	His Honor Mayor Cragin stated that by reason of the fact that the Board had

Govt Lot 2, Sec. 2	received a request from the parties making the appeal for the rezoning of

T 21 S	Government Lot 2, Section 2, T 21 S, R 61 E, MDB&M from R-1 to R-4, for

	additional time before this decision is rendered, no action would be taken

at this time.

Mr. C.L. Martin verified this request and asked that this matter be held
in abeyance until a week from Wednesday.

Mr. William Shearer, one of the property owners protesting this proposed
rezoning stated he could see no reason for the delay in this matter, and
would like an answer today.

Thereafter Commissioner Moore moved the aforementioned matter be held in
abeyance until Wednesday October 11, 1950 at the hour of 3:30 P.M.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting
aye: noes, none.

STATE BUILDING - Request	Commissioner Peccole moved that inasmuch as public opinion is against

for location in City Park	locating the State Building on City Park site, the Board go on record

as opposing the granting of a site in the City Park for the State
Building. Further the Board recommends that Harrison Stocks be contacted.
for his site located approximately at 19th Street on Fremont.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote:
Commissioners Bunker, Moore, Peccole, Whipple and His Honor voting aye;
noes, none.
