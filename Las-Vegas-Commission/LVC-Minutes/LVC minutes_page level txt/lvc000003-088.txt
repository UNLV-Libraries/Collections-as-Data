OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK, CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

April 3rd, 1930.

At a regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas held on
the 3rd day of April 1930 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

Present Mayor Hesse, Commissioners Thomas, Hansell, Smith, and German together with the
City Attorney and Clerk.

All bills allowed as per "Claims and Warrant Register" on file in the office of the
Clerk.

Monies received for the month of March as follows:

$1060.00 Fines.

2035.00	Donation to Library Fund.

864.95	Licenses, fees sale of cemetery lots.

3959.95	Total.

Monies proportioned as follows:

$2,035.00 Library fund

84.00	Water, Sewer & Light Fund.

30.00	Cemetery Fund.

1,810.95	Police & Fire Fund.

3,959.95	Total

Money transferred: $500.00 from General Fund to Police and Fire Fund.

Upon motion duly made seconded and carried the following gaming licenses were granted,
same having been approved by Police and Fire Commissioner Thomas, and money to cover said
licenses being paid to the Clerk at the time said applications were made.

Licenses granted as follows: Leo, Kind, Sanders & Hodges, J. R. Boyer, F. S. Le Roy, Gilmore
and Horden, A. De Vorin, and Wm. Jones each for two tables, and A. T. Mc Carter, and the
Boulder Club for three tables.

Motion introduced by Commissioner Thomas, seconded by Commissioner Hansell, vote on said
motion was as follows: Commissioners Thomas, German, Hansell, and Smith, and His Honor the
Mayor J. F. Hesse, voting Aye. Nones None.

Upon motion of Commissioner Hansell seconded by Commissioner Thomas it was moved that
the resignation of F. A. Thomason be accepted, and that R. H. Elser be appointed Plumbing
Inspector, to furnish a bond of One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00)

Vote on said motion was as follows: Commissioners Thomas, German, Hansell, and Smith,
and His Honor the Mayor voting Aye. Noes none.

Upon motion of Commissioner Hansell, seconded by Commissioner German it was moved that
if Plumbing Permit No. 101 was not found same to be cancelled.

Said permit was lost tract of during the change in the office of Plumbing Inspector.

Vote on said motion was as follows: Commissioner Thomas, German, Hansell, and Smith
and His Honor the Mayor voting Aye. Noes None.

Upon motion of Commissioner Thomas, seconded by Commissioner German it was moved that
the sum of $50.00 be paid Marjorie Goodwin from the Police and Fire Fund, as she had put
in considerable time in the Police Dept. during the month of March.

Vote on said motion was as follows: Commissioner Thomas, German, Hansell, and Smith, and
His Honor the Mayor J. F. Hesse voting Aye. Noes None.

At this time the Board took up the matter of providing for a special City Election at
which should be submitted the question as to whether Mayor J. F. Hesse should be recalled
from such office, the City Clerk having issued a call for such election to be held on
Wednesday the 23rd day of April 1930.
