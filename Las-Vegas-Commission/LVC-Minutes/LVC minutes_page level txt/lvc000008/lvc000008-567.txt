Mr. Edwin S. Lowe, representing the proposed Glendale Gardens project in the request to connect
to and use the City Sewage facilities, was present and discussed this matter with the Board.

Thereafter Commissioner Bunker moved the request of Glendale Gardens to use the sewage
facilities of the City be denied.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp and Whipple voting aye; noes, none. Not voting:	Mayor Baker.

Commissioner Whipple moved that the Board go on record as being willing to negotiate sewer
service contracts with applicants in the County if the applicants are adjacent to the City
and are willing to request annexation and secure consent from the County, within a reasonable
time, providing that they have immediate plans for the development of their project.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Whipple moved that the Board act as soon as possible to acquire additional ground
and easements for sewer lines and for the purpose of building a new sewage disposal plant to
take care of growth and expansion of the City.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners Bunker

Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time, Commissioner Whipple
moved this meeting adjourn.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners Bunker

Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye, noes, none.
