Mayor Baker requested that Commissioner Sharp show the proposed plans for the improve-
ment of the circle in Hyde Park to the property owners that are present who have
petitioned the City for the beautification of this property. Mayor Baker explained
that provision had been made in the new Budget for this improvement but that the
City would not know whether the work could be accomplished until the Budget is approved
in April.

Mrs. Dave Brown, spokesman for the group was asked to appoint a committee to work with
the City on this project.

Commissioner Bunker moved the Director of Public Works be instructed to proceed to
prepare plans and specifications and advertise for bids to be opened March 6, 1957 for
the installation of a sprinkling system in the circle in Hyde Park.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Planning Director Franklin Bills presented the recommendation of the Traffic & Parking
Commission that one-hour parking requirements be placed on the north side of Ogden
between 7th and 8th Streets.

Commissioner Fountain moved this recommendation be held in abeyance until the City obtains
written indication from the property owners as to whether they are in favor of this change.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Fountain, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

The Commission considered the recommendation of the Traffic and Parking Commission
that the Chief of Police be delegated authority to install two-hour parking limits on
both sides of South Main Street from Bridger to South 5th Street.

This recommendation was held in abeyance until property owners and tenants in the area
can be contacted and indicate in writing whether they are in favor of a two-hour park-
ing limit.

At this time the City Engineer presented the request of Standard Wholesale Supply Company
that the agreement for the installation of off-site improvements in connection with the
use permit granted them to install a bulk plant and service station east of their property
at 855 West Bonanza be assigned to Union Oil Company. Mr. Wade explained that this
property had been sold to Union Oil Company, and there was no agreement required for the
installation of off-site improvements of Standard Wholesale since their building was
developed under conforming use.
