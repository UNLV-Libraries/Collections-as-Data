OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK CITY OF
LAS VEGAS, CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.
DECEMBER 8th, 1938

Minutes of a recessed regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas
held on the 8th day of December, 1938 at the hour of seven o'clock P.M.

Present Mayor Marble, Commissioners Ronnow, Luce, Krause and Corradetti, City Attorney and
City Clerk

Attorney Ham appeared before the Board and ask that the location of the Overland Bar be
held open for operation as a Tavern since the fore closure of said tavern by the owners of the
building. Said foreclose affecting the license granted to Harry Anderson for operation in said
Building. The Clerk read to the Board a letter from Mr. Anderson asking that he be permitted to
transfer said license to the Northern Club since the foreclosure on the location held by him in
the Overland Hotel.

Mayor Marble informed the interested parties that this matter would be considered at a
meeting to be held in the near future.

This being the time set for hearing the cases of the Rose Distributing Company, Peter
Pansey and Ethel Rapoport and a large number of interested persons being present per mission
was secured from the Sheriff to hold the meeting in the County Court House. Thereupon on motion
of Commissioner Luce seconded by Commissioner Corradetti it mas moved and carried that the
meeting adjourn to the District Court Room in the County Court House.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Luce, Krause, and Corradetti and His Honor the Mayor voting-
aye. Noes, none.

There upon the meeting recessed to convene in the Court Room.

Mayor Marble opened the meeting and informed those present that this was a hearing and
in no way to be conducted as a court.

Mrs. Malon Brown was present to take down and prepare transcript of said meeting which
is to be filed in the Clerk's Office for reference.

Mr. John Cahlan as Probation Officer questioned Clinton Goodwin, a minor, as to where
and when liquors were purchased by himself other minors.

On motion of Commissioner Ronnow seconded by Commissioner Corradetti it was moved and
carried that Ethel Rapoport be excused from any questioning at this time as no evidence had
been presented showing the sale of liquors from her place of business to minors.

Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Luce, Krause and Corradetti and His Honor the Mayor voting

aye.

Mr. Pansey was not present for questioning being absent from the City this date.

After some questioning Clinton Goodwin stated positively that John Rose of the
Rose Distributing Company had sold him wine. Mr. Rose denied this.

No definite action was taken on this matter, revoking or canceling any licenses.

On motion of Commissioner Luce Seconded By Commissioner Ronnow it was moved and carried
that the meeting recess and adjourn until Wednesday the 14th day of December, 1938 at the hour
of seven o'clock P.M. Vote Commissioners Ronnow, Luce, Krause and Corradetti and His Honor
the Mayor voting aye. Noes, none.

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK CITY OF
LAS VEGAS CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

DECEMBER 14th, 1938

Minutes of a recessed regular meeting of the Board of commissioners of the City of
Las Vegas Clark County, nevada held on the 14th day of December, .1938 at the hour of seven
o'clock P.M.

Present Mayor Marble, Commissioners Ronnow, Luce and Krause City Clerk and City Attorney.

Absent Commissioner Corradetti.

This being the time when Ordinance No. 244 should came before the Board for second read-
ing and adoption said ordinance entitled, "AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS LICENSING
FOR THE PURPOSE OF REGULATION AND REVENUE EVERY KIND OF LAWFUL BUSINESS HEREINAFTER SPECIFIED
TRANSACTED OR CARRIED ON WITHIN THE CORPORATE LIMITS OF THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS, STATE OF NEVADA
FIXING THE RATES OF LICENSE TAX UPON THE SAME AND PROVIDING FOR THE COLLECTION OF SAID LICENSE
TAX, AND A PUNISHMENT FOR CARRYING ON OR CONDUCTING ANY SUCH BUSINESS WITHOUT A LICENSE AND
REPEALING ORDINANCES NUMBERED 1,2,4,13,14,35, 48, 62, 74,75, 81, 95, 102, 105, 108, 112, 117,
145, 184, 192, and 243" was read for the second time and adopted by the following vote: Com-
missioners Luce and Krause and His Honor the Mayor H.P. Marble voting aye. Commissioner Ron-
now voting No. Absent Commissioner Corradetti.

At this time Mayor Marble instructed the Clerk to read to the Board a statement of Att-
orney Marshall regarding the sale of liquors to minors and the hearing on said case held at a
recessed regular meeting of the Board December 8th. Said report was ordered filed with the
Clerk.

The Board set the date of December 21st 1938 as the time to hear the charges against
Peter Pansey. Said charges being the sale of liquors to minors.

Mr. White of the State Health Board was present and informed the Board that his depart-
ment would cooperate in every possible in health and sanitation problems in the City.
