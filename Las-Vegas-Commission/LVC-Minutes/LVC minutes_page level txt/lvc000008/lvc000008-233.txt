This being the time heretofore set for the Public Hearing on the recommended vacation of a portion of the
semicircle north of New York Avenue that falls west of the northerly prolongation of the West Side of
Fairfield Place, His Honor asked if there were any persons present who wished to object to this vacation.
There were no objections voiced.

Thereafter Commissioner Whipple moved the recommendation of the Regional Planning Commission that this
vacation be approved be adopted and the following order adopted.

A Petition, dated the 11th day of September, 1952, signed by five
(5) freeholders residing in the area affected, having been filed with the Clerk of this
Board, petitioning for the vacation of certain streets and parts of Streets in the Meadows
Addition hereinafter described, and said Petition having been by Order of this Board referred to
the Planning Commission of the City of Las Vegas for its recommendation in the premises, and
said Planning Commission having filed its report, dated November, 13, 1952, with the Board
approving and recommending said Vacation;

And this Board, by an Order made at tis regular meeting held on the 3rd day of December,

1952, set the 7th day of January, 1953, at the hour of 7:30 P.M. at the Commissioners'

Room of the Board of Commissioners, in the War Memorial Building, located at the Northwest
corner of Fifth and Stewart Streets in the City of Las Vegas, Clark County, Nevada, as the
time and place for the Public Hearing on said Petition and recommendation, and ordered the
City Clerk to cause the aforesaid Street and parts of street proposed to be vacated to be
posted with a notice setting forth the time and place of the Public Hearing and the extent
of the proposed vacation;
