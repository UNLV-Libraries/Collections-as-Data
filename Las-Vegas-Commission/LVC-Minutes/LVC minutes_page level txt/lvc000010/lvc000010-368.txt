NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that this Association go on record as favoring an
amendment to the present law providing that all sales of materials to contractors for
incorporation into public improvements by contract with State, County or City Govern-
ments be exempt from paying sales tax thereon.

III-	Now, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that this Association go on record as favoring an
amendment to the State Budget Law to provide for the following matters:

A.	That a uniform Budget Act be adopted to apply uniformly to County, City and School
Districts.

B.	To provide that the Budget of County, City and School Districts be submitted as of
April 1st of each year.

C.	To permit fund transfers subject to the approval of the appropriate state agencies
without requiring an emergency loan when the appropriate agency has funds from
which transfers could be made.

D.	That legislations be enacted allowing incorporated cities to set aside funds each
year to be carried over as a sinking fund for the purpose of making future capital
expenditures.

IV-	NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that this Association go on record as favoring legis-
lation requiring persons accepting employment within the State to be considered as
residents for the purpose of licensing of motor vehicles.

V-NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the State law be amended to require the Attorney
General of Nevada to give written opinions to municipalities upon request on matters
involving interpretation of State statutes. VI-NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that legis-
lation be enacted providing that automobile licenses shall be issued: to the owner of a vehicle

and remain with the owner upon transfer of title to such vehicle. VII- WHEREAS, there is a
conflict in the present law governing Cities, in that some Cities are governed by General
Statutes and some by Special Charters.

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that this Association secure legislation providing
that amendments to the general law governing municipalities shall not apply to Cities
incorporated under charter provisions when the charters thereof contains legislation
on the same subject.

VIII-NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, that the Nevada Municipal Association go on record as
favoring the adoption of permissive legislation authorizing Nevada Cities to establish
an urban re-development agency if they find need for such an agency and so desire.
