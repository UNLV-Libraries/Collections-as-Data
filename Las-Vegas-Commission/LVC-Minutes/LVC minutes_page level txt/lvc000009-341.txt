Mr. Trent stated that these items would cost approximately $10,000.

Thereafter Commissioner Bunker moved this request be approved subject to there being funds
available.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

City Attorney Howard W. Cannon reported that he had written to all the Strip Hotels,

North Las Vegas and Sherwood Park concerning sewer service. He stated that only the El Rancho
Vegas had answered his letter, and had sent a check to pay their sewer rental service until
January 1, 1955.

City Attorney Howard W. Cannon reported that Nevada Electric, Contractor on Assessment
District No. 100-25, had informed him that they have an extension of time on the poles
until December 1, 1954. He also stated that Mr. Manente of the First National Bank
had told him that they could wait until January 1, 1955 to take delivery on the bonds.

Chief of Police A. H. Kennedy requested that the Board call for bids for a contract for
towing service for the Police Department.

Thereafter Commissioner Whipple moved that these bids be opened on December 1, 1954 at
7:30 P.M.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time, Commissioner Sharp
moved that this meeting adjourn.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.
