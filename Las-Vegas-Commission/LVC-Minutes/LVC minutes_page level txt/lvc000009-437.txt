Las Vegas, Nevada
February 28, 1955

(CALL OF SPECIAL MEETING)

A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada held
this 28th day of February, 1955 was called to order at the hour of 7:30 P.M. by His
Honor Mayor Baker with the following members present:

Mayor	C. D. Baker

Commissioner	Wendell Bunker

Commissioner	Harris P. Sharp

Commissioner	Reed Whipple

City Manager	George	H.	Rounthwaite

Assistant City Manager	Robert	W.	Notti

City Attorney	Howard	W.	Cannon

City Engineer	George	E.	Wade

City Clerk	Shirley Ballinger

Absent:	Commissioner	Rex A. Jarrett

Discussion was held on the City Budget for 1955, and it was determined that there is
an excess of approximately $43,000.00 more requested than there is estimated available
funds.

Commissioner Bunker moved that Commissioner Whipple be delegated to work over and
balance the budget.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Sharp and lost by the following vote:	Commissioners

Bunker and Sharp voting aye; noes, Commissioner Whipple and Mayor Baker. Absent:
Commissioner Jarrett.

Thereafter the Commission instructed the Assistant City Manager and the Director of
Finance to balance the budget and have it ready for approval March 2, 1955 at 7:00 P.M.

Commissioner Bunker moved that the Municipal Election be held May 3, 1955 and the
following proclamation adopted:

WHEREAS, it appears that the Charter of the City of Las Vegas provides that
a General. Election be held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in May, 1955;
in the City of Las Vegas, for the election at large by the qualified voters of the
City of a Mayor, two Commissioners of the first and third class, respectively, and a
Judge of the Municipal Court,

NOW, THEREFORE, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED by the Board of Commissioners of the City
of Las Vegas, that such General Election be held in the City of Las Vegas on May 3; 1955;
at which election there shall be elected at large by the qualified voters of said City,
a Mayor who shall be elected and hold office for a period of four years and until his
successor is elected and qualified; two Commissioners, one of the first class and one
of the third class, who shall be elected and hold office for a period of four years
and until their successors are elected and qualified; and a Judge of the Municipal
Court, who shall be elected and hold office for a period of four years and until his
successor shall be elected and qualified, and

IT IS FURTHER ORDERED, that the places in said City of holding said election
