ORDINANCE no. 180.

An Ordinance providing for the issuance of bonds of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada,
in the amount of One Hundred Sixty Thousand Dollars, ($160,000.00) for the purpose of 
constructing main sanitary outfall sewers and a sewage disposal plant in addition to and also
as an extension of the present sanitary sewer system in said City, prescribing the form
of said bonds and the interest coupons to be thereto attached, and in making provisions
for the levy of a tax to pay the interest as the installments thereof fall due and to
pay the principal at maturity.

Whereas, pursuant to and in strict compliance with the provisions and authority
of the Constitution and Laws of the State of Nevada, including the provisions of Chapter
132 of the laws of 1911 of the State of Nevada, being an Act of the Senate and Assembly
of the State Of Nevada, entitled, "An act to incorporate the town of Las Vegas, in Clark
County, and defining the boundaries thereof, and to authorize the establishing of a city
government therefor, and other matters relating thereto," approved March 16, 1911, and
all acts and parts amendatory thereof and supplemental thereto, the City of Las Vegas was

authorized to issue its bonds for the purposes of constructing a sewerage system (including

 a disposal plant) within said city and waste mains therefrom; and Whereas, pursuant
to each of the requirements of subsection 5 of Section 31 of said Chapter 132 of the laws
of 191.1 of the State of Nevada, as amended by Section 3 of Chapter 215 of the laws of 1931
of the State of Nevada, a special election by the regularly qualified electors of the City
of Las Vegas was duly called and held to determine whether or not bonds of said City in
the total sum of One Hundred Sixty Thousand Dollars ($160,000.00) as hereinafter described
shall be issued, notice of which election having been first duly given strictly as therein
provided, at which election more than a majority of the votes cast were in favor of the
issuance of said bonds; and Whereas, said bonds are being duly advertised for sale, such
sale to take place on the 14th day of October, 1931 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.; and

Whereas, all conditions precedent to the lawful issuance of said bonds have happened

and been duly observed in all things strictly as required by law, therefore,

THE BOARD OF CITY COMMISSIONERS 0F THE CITY OF LAS VEGAS DO ORDAIN;

Section l. That for the purposes aforesaid there shall be and there are hereby ordered
and directed to be issued to the purchaser or purchasers at said sale the negotiable coupon
 bonds of said City in the aggregate amount of One Hundred Sixty Thousand Dollars
($160,000.00). Said bonds shall be designated "Las Vegas Sewerage Bonds, 1931 issue," and
shall be in the denomination of One Thousand Dollars ($1000.00) each, numbered one to
one hundred sixty (1-160) inclusive, dated July 1, 1931, and mature and become payable as
follows: Bonds numbered 1 to 8 inclusive shall become due and payable on the first day of

the first day of July 1937; bonds numbered 41 to 48 inclusive shall become due and payable
on the first day of July, 1938; bonds numbered 49 to 56 inclusive shall become due and payable
on the first day of July, 1939; bonds numbered 57 to 64 inclusive shall become due and
payable on the first day of July, 1940; bonds numbered 65 to 72 inclusive shall become due
and payable on the first day of July 1941; bonds numbered 73 to 80 inclusive shall become
due and payable on the first day of July, 1942; bonds numbered 81 to 88 inclusive shall
become due and payable on the first day of July 1943; bonds numbered 89 to 96 shall become
due and payable on the first day of July 1944; bonds numbered 97 to 104 shall become due
and payable on the on the first day of July, 1945; bonds numbered 105 to 112 inclusive
shall become due and payable on the first day of July, 1946; bonds numbered 113 to 120
inclusive shall become due and payable on the first day of July, 1947; bonds numbered 121
to 128 inclusive shall become due and payable on the first day of July 1948; bonds numbered
129 to 136 inclusive shall become due and payable on the first day of July, 1949; bonds
