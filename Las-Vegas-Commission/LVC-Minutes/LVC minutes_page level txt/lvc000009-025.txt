ACCEPTANCE OF NOTICE

We, the undersigned Mayor, Commissioners, City Manager and City Attorney of the
City of Las Vegas, Nevada, do hereby admit due service of the foregoing notice of
Special Meeting.

Las Vegas, Nevada
March 5, 1954

A special meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, Nevada held
this 5th day of March, 1954 was called to order at the hour of 4:00 P.M. by His Honor
Mayor Baker with the following members present:

Mayor	C. D. Baker

Commissioner	Wendell Bunker

Commissioner	Rex A. Jarrett

Commissioner	Haris P. Sharp

Commissioner	Reed Whipple

City Attorney	Howard W. Cannon

City Manager	George Rounthwaite

City Clerk	Shirley Ballinger

At this time a memorandum from the City Attorney regarding the condemnation of A Street
was presented and discussed.

Thereafter Commissioner Sharp moved the City abandon the condemnation proceedings on
A Street.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Whipple and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Discussion was held on granting the Police Department a step advance in salary and doing
away with their holidays.

Thereafter Commissioner Bunker moved this matter be referred to the City Attorney to
amend the ordinance to provide for the deletion of holidays and that a step raise in s
salary be granted to the Police Department.

Motion seconded by Commissioner Jarrett and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

Commissioner Jarrett moved that effective March 15, 1954, salaries of the following
appointive personnel be adjusted as follows:

City Manager (Acting)        $850.00 per month
Assistant to City Manager     600.00 per month
Director of Public Works	      800.00 per month
Chief of Police	675.00	per	month

Fire Chief	600.00	per	month

 Comptroller	600.00	per	month

Motion seconded by Commissioner Bunker and carried by the following vote: Commissioners

Bunker, Jarrett, Sharp, Whipple and His Honor voting aye; noes, none.

There being no further business to come before this Board at this time, the meeting
adjourned.
