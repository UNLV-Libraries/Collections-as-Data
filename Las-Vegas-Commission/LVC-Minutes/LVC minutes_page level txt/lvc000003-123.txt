OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK, CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

OCTOBER 2nd, 1930.

At a regular meeting of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Las Vegas, held on
the 2nd day of October 1930 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

Present Mayor Hesse, Commissioners Thomas, and Smith together with the City Attorney
and Clerk.

Absent Commissioners German, and Hansell.

Commissioner Smith was appointed by the Mayor to approve the bills of the finance
officer owning to the absence of Commissioner German Finance Commissioner.

All bills allowed as per Claims and Warrant register on file in the office of the

Clerk.

Monies received for the month of September as follows:

$160.00 Municipal Court Fines.

1670.00	Licenses, fees, and sale of Cemetery lots.

1830.00	Total.

Monies proportioned as follows:

$1,076.00 Police and Fire Fund

650.00 Street Fund

60.00	Cemetery Fund

44.00	Water. Sewer Light Fund.

1,830.00

Mr. Ward, Mr. Flowers, Mr. Down, and Captain Griffin of the Salvation Army appeared
before the Board at this time in regards to the carring for of the needy in the City at
the present time, and ask that the Board of Commissioners donate a substantial sum toward
the cause.

City Attorney Stevens informed them that at the present time the City could not
donate towards the fund as the only fund that the City could donate from was the Publicity
Fund and at the present time that fund depleted, and would not be reimbursed until the City
had received its share from the taxes in December.

An Ordinance entitled, "An Ordinance for licensing and regulating the carrying
on of certain businesses, professions, trades, callings, and occupations; fixing a penalty
for the violation thereof; repealing ordinances of the City of Las Vegas, as follows:
Ordinance No. 48, approved December 3, 1913, Ordinance No. 62 adopted December 1, 1915,
Ordinance No. 74, adopte d March 5, 1919, Ordinance No. 75, adopted May 7, 1919, Ordinance
No. 81, Adopted June 15, 1921, Ordinance No. 95, adopted September 6, 1922, Ordinance
No. 102, approved November 26, 1923, and all ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict
herewith," was read to the Board for a first time, and laid over for a final reading.

No further business appearing before the Board at this time the meeting recessed until
Tuesday October 7th at the hour of three o'clock P.M.
