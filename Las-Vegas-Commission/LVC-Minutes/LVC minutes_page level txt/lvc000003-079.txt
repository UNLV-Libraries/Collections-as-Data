OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK, CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

FEBRUARY 26th, 1930.

At a recessed regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners held on the 26th day of
February 1930 at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

Present Mayor Hesse, Commissioners Hansell, German, Smith, and Thomas together with the
City Attorney and Clerk.

Minutes of meeting of February 21st read and approved as read.

Upon motion of Commissioner Smith, seconded by Commissioner Thomas it was moved that a
map of the "Resubdivision of of Block 13, and portions of Blocks 21 and 22 of bardie addition
to the City of Las Vegas, Clark County, Nevada," Owner Miller-Buzick Corp. be approved by the
Board of City Commissioners. That the same be Approved by the Mayor J.F. Hesse, and Attest
by City Clerk Viola Burns, under the seal of the City of Las Vegas.

Vote on said motion was as follows: Commissioners Smith, Thomas, German, and Hansell,
and His Honor the Mayor Hesse, voting Aye. Noes None.

At this time the members entered into a discussion on the City Budget for the coming
year. No decision was reached at this time.

There being no further business to come before the Board at this time the meeting re-
cessed until Friday the 28th day of February at the hour of three o'clock P.M.

OFFICE OF THE CITY CLERK, CITY OF LAS VEGAS,
CLARK COUNTY, NEVADA.

FEBRUARY 28th, 1930.

At a recessed regular meeting of the Board of City Commissioners of the City of Las
Vegas, held on the 28th day of February 1930 at the hour of seven thirty P.M.

Present Mayor Hesse, Commissioners German, and Smith together with the City Attorney
and Clerk.

Absent Commissioners Thomas, and Hansell.

The following Resolution was introduced by Commissioners Smith seconded by Commissioner
German:

Resolved, that the Commissioners of Streets O.J. Smith be and he is hereby authorized
and empowered to enter into a contract with the "Autin-Western Road Machinery Co;" to pur-
chase 1-Model 20 Austin Dual Drive Motor Grader complete with special twelve foot Blade, ten
inch rear tires and Cab."

Vote on said Resolution was as follows; Commissioners German, and Smith, and His Honor
the Mayor Hesse, voting Aye. Noes none.
